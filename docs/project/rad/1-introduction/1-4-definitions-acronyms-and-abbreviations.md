# OhMyUniversity! - RAD: 1.4 Definitions, Acronyms, and Abbreviations

This glossary provides a comprehensive reference for the technical terms, institutional systems, and regulatory frameworks utilized throughout this document, ensuring a shared understanding across all project stakeholders:

* **AgID (Agenzia per l'Italia Digitale)**: The technical agency that establishes mandatory guidelines for the accessibility and usability of digital services in the Italian public sector.
* **API (Application Programming Interface)**: A structured set of procedures and tools allowing the system to securely exchange data with university servers.
* **CFU (Credito Formativo Universitario)**: A unit measuring student workload, where 1 CFU conventionally corresponds to 25 hours of work.
* **Cineca / Esse3**: An inter-university consortium and management system (Esse3) used by Italian universities to monitor student careers, exams, and fees.
* **Corporate Partners**: External companies and entities that enable the system to provide students with dedicated conventions, cultural events, and discount codes.
* **Degree Base Grade**: A numerical value representing the projected starting grade for the final exam, calculated on a 110-point scale (/110).
* **EDP (European Data Portal)**: The central European infrastructure (data.europa.eu) that provides access to open data from European public administrations, used for integrating regional and national public services.
* **GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)**: EU Regulation 2016/679 regarding the strict protection and processing of users' personal and sensitive data.
* **GOMP**: A proprietary management system integrated to manage academic records and student careers.
* **Honors (Lode)**: A merit mention for a 30/30 grade that may add additional value to weighted average calculations depending on specific regulations.
* **Map Providers**: External services (such as Google Maps or OpenStreetMap) that support the system’s geolocation, campus navigation, and transportation features.
* **Middleware**: Software that acts as an intermediary layer between institutional databases and the final user interface.
* **MIM (Ministero dell’Istruzione e del Merito)**: The Italian Ministry responsible for the primary and secondary education system, often providing data for incoming university freshmen.
* **Moodle**: An external learning platform integrated to provide direct access to course materials and teaching resources.
* **MUR (Ministero dell’Università e della Ricerca)**: The Italian Ministry of University and Research, the central governing body for the national higher education system.
* **SPID / CIE**: National digital identity systems (OIDC/SAML protocols) supported for secure user authentication.
* **Synchronization**: The process of updating local application data to reflect the most recent state available on official university servers.
